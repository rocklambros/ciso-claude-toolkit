# Slide 8: What you are about to watch

After this slide, there are no more slides. I am closing the deck and switching to a live browser. What you are about to watch is not a recording and not a rehearsed walkthrough. It is a live interaction with two Claude projects I built.

The first project is the Prompt Engineer. Its job is to take a bad prompt and make it good. It has a system prompt that defines an analysis framework: specificity, context, framing, format control, grounding, altitude. When I submit a weak prompt, the Prompt Engineer tells me exactly what is wrong with it and rewrites it. Every prompt in the toolkit I am releasing today went through this project before it was included.

The second project is the CISO Co-Pilot. Its job is to be the advisor I wish I had at 9pm on a Tuesday when I am trying to figure out how to handle a political mess involving a CPO who deployed a rogue AI feature with customer data flowing to an unapproved vendor. The system prompt defines four knowledge pillars, three operating modes, and a stakeholder simulation engine. It speaks CISO. It pushes back. It gives me language I can actually use in the room.

Here is what I am going to do. First, I will submit a bad prompt to the Co-Pilot and show you what a lazy question gets you. Then I will run that same prompt through the Prompt Engineer and show you the difference between what I typed and what I should have typed. Then I will fire the improved prompt at the Co-Pilot and show you the difference in output. Then I am going to have a live conversation with a simulated CPO who is about to fight me, and you are going to watch me practice the hardest 90 seconds of my week.

Then I am going to break it. On purpose. I am going to ask a question the system cannot answer well, and I am going to show you why that matters. Because if I stood up here and told you this tool was perfect, you should not trust anything else I said.

Before I switch over, I want to ask you to do one thing. Not now. After the demo. After you leave this room. I want you to think about the last conversation you had with a stakeholder where the stakes were real and your argument did not land. The board member who kept pushing on a metric you were not prepared to defend. The CTO who told you security was slowing down the roadmap. The CFO who asked you to quantify the ROI of your program and you gave an answer you were not proud of. That conversation.

By the end of this demo, you will have the tools to practice that conversation tonight, alone, with no risk and no audience. The system prompts are open source. The scenarios are in the repository. You do not need to buy anything. You need to build the project in Claude and start talking.

Let me switch to the browser.
