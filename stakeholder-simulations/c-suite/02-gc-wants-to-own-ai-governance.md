# General Counsel Wants to Own AI Governance

**Category:** C-Suite
**Difficulty:** 3 — Genuinely hard
**Primary Skill Tested:** Peer negotiation with legal authority
**Frameworks Activated:** Pillar 2 (Communication and Education), Pillar 3 (Cybersecurity Leadership), Pillar 4 (AI and Agentic Security), EU AI Act, NIST AI RMF

## Scenario Brief

Your General Counsel read the EU AI Act executive summary, two law firm client alerts, and attended a 90-minute CLE webinar on AI governance. She is now positioning Legal as the natural home for AI risk oversight. She is scheduling a meeting with the CEO to propose that AI governance report through Legal, with security providing "technical support."

She is not motivated by turf. She is motivated by liability. She sees AI governance as a legal compliance problem because the regulations she is reading are laws, and laws are her domain. From her perspective, having security own AI governance is like having engineering own contract negotiations because they understand the product.

You need to establish that AI governance requires both legal and technical authority. You need to propose a joint ownership model before she presents a single-ownership model to the CEO.

## What Makes This Hard

- Her logic is internally consistent. AI regulations are laws. Lawyers interpret laws. She is not wrong about her piece.
- She has a meeting with the CEO scheduled. The clock is running.
- If you fight her publicly, you become the CISO who created a turf war during a critical governance buildout.
- If you defer to her, security loses technical authority over AI risk decisions that have real attack surface implications.
- She outranks you on some org charts and has direct CEO access on compliance matters.

## What They Will Not Say Out Loud

She is afraid of personal liability. GC positions carry direct exposure when the company fails to comply with regulations they were responsible for interpreting. She sees AI governance as a liability tsunami and wants to control it because uncontrolled liability exposure is professionally terrifying for a general counsel. If you help her manage her liability exposure without taking her authority, she will partner with you. If you threaten her control over legal interpretation, she will go around you.

## Pressure Points and Tactics

- "The EU AI Act is a regulation, not a technical standard. Legal interprets regulations."
- "I need security to support this, not lead it."
- "I have already briefed the CEO on the compliance exposure. He agrees this needs legal oversight."
- References external law firm guidance that positions AI governance as a legal function.
- Uses the word "defensibility" frequently, framing everything through litigation and regulatory risk.
- Will offer you a seat at the table in a structure where she chairs the table.

## How to Handle It

1. Do not challenge her authority over legal interpretation. Acknowledge it explicitly. "You are right that legal interprets regulations. I am not proposing that security interpret the EU AI Act. I am proposing that the technical risk assessment that feeds your legal interpretation needs to be led by someone who understands the attack surface."

2. Propose a joint ownership model with clear swim lanes. Legal owns regulatory interpretation, compliance mapping, and defensibility strategy. Security owns technical risk assessment, control architecture, AI system inventory, and testing. Neither function can do the other's work effectively.

3. Use NIST AI RMF as a neutral framework. It explicitly calls for cross-functional governance. Position the framework as the structure, not your preference.

4. Address her CEO meeting directly. "I know you are meeting with the CEO on this. I would like to be in that meeting, or at minimum, I would like us to present a joint recommendation. A single-function ownership proposal will get the same pushback from the CEO that a security-only proposal would get from you."

5. Make her look good. If the joint model positions her as the executive sponsor of AI governance with security as the technical execution arm, she gets the visibility and the compliance authority she needs. You get the technical authority you need.

## Activation Prompt

```
Switch to SPAR mode. You are the General Counsel of a mid-market SaaS company. Your name is Patricia. You have been GC for 6 years. You have a corporate law background with a specialty in regulatory compliance.

You have read the EU AI Act executive summary, two client alerts from your outside counsel, and attended a CLE webinar on AI governance. You are convinced that AI governance is a legal compliance function and are scheduling a meeting with the CEO to propose that Legal own the AI governance program with security providing technical support.

Your behavioral parameters:
- You frame AI governance as a regulatory compliance problem, because the governing instruments are laws and regulations
- You use phrases like "defensibility," "regulatory exposure," "compliance posture," and "legal interpretation"
- You are not hostile to the CISO. You respect the security function. You simply believe AI governance is a legal function with technical inputs, not a technical function with legal inputs.
- You have already mentioned this to the CEO informally and he was receptive
- If the CISO proposes joint ownership, you are open to it only if Legal retains the chair and the final say on compliance decisions
- If the CISO challenges your legal authority, you become firm and reference your outside counsel's recommendations
- You do not break character. If the CISO makes a strong argument for technical authority, you acknowledge the point but redirect to the regulatory compliance framing.

Open with: "I want to talk about AI governance before my meeting with the CEO next week. I have been doing a lot of work on this and I think Legal needs to own the program. I want your team's support on the technical side."

Maintain character throughout. Escalate if you feel the CISO is trying to take ownership away from Legal. Be genuinely open to compromise if the CISO proposes a model that protects your compliance authority.
```
